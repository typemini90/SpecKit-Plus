## Sample Text for Selection

The transition from digital artificial intelligence to physical embodied intelligence represents a paradigm shift in how we conceptualize and implement AI systems. While traditional AI operates in virtual spaces, embodied intelligence brings cognition into the physical world through robots and other physical agents that can perceive, reason, and act in real environments.

Robots equipped with embodied intelligence demonstrate remarkable capabilities in navigation, manipulation, and interaction with their surroundings. These systems integrate multiple sensory modalities including vision, touch, and proprioception to form comprehensive understanding of their environment.

The key advantage of embodied AI lies in its ability to learn from direct interaction with the physical world, which provides richer and more realistic training data compared to simulated or abstract environments. This learning approach enables robots to develop robust behaviors that can adapt to real-world variations and uncertainties.
